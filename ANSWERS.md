**5. How does employee tenure vary across different departments and job roles, and are there any patterns that suggest potential issues with career progression or employee satisfaction in specific areas?**

The "Tenure by Department and Role" heatmap revealed distinct patterns in employee longevity:

* **IT/IS Department:** Roles within IT/IS generally exhibit shorter average tenures, ranging from **1.48 to 3.85 years**. This suggests a potentially competitive external market or internal factors like workload or limited growth opportunities contributing to quicker turnover.
* **Executive Office:** Employees in the Executive Office tend to have significantly longer tenures, often **exceeding 6.10 years**. While indicative of stability and potentially high job satisfaction, this could also signal limited internal mobility within this group.
* **Production Department:** Tenure in Production shows variability. While roles like "Production Manager" average around **4.58 years**, "Production Technician I" has a similar average of **4.96 years**. This unexpected similarity in tenure between a management and entry-level role might point to issues with career progression or satisfaction in the technician role.
* **Sales Department:** Tenure varies across Sales roles. "Area Sales Manager" averages around **5.20 years**, while "Director of Sales" (**4.26 years**) and "Sales Manager" (**3.65 years**) show shorter average tenures, potentially reflecting different career trajectories or pressures associated with these positions.

**Potential Issues and Considerations:**

* The consistently shorter tenure in IT/IS warrants further investigation into factors affecting employee satisfaction and career growth compared to industry norms.
* The long tenure in the Executive Office could be leveraged for mentorship and exploring internal mobility opportunities.
* The similar tenure between "Production Manager" and "Production Technician I" suggests a need to examine career paths and satisfaction for entry-level production roles.
* The varying tenures within the Sales department could be further understood through analyzing role-specific pressures and career progression opportunities.
